Apple Watch + Christy Turlington Burns

I’m using Apple Watch to train for one of the world’s biggest marathons. Wish me luck! And check back each week to see how it’s going.

Christy Turlington Burns, one of the world’s top models, is the founder of Every Mother Counts, a nonprofit dedicated to making pregnancy and childbirth safe for every mother. Christy currently serves on the Harvard Medical School Global Health Council and as an advisor to the Harvard School of Public Health Board of Dean’s Advisors. She is the director of the film No Woman, No Cry, a documentary about the dire maternal health conditions that impact millions of women around the world. As a result of her advocacy work, she was named one of Time’s 100 Most Influential People in 2014. Christy, a three-time marathon finisher, lives in New York with her husband and children.

Week Two Back in NYC

It feels great to be back in New York. I’d been gone for almost two weeks by the time I returned from Africa, so I put the Modular face on my Apple Watch right away to help me get back into the swing of things. It’s been great to see my calendar, the temperature, my Activity rings, and my next appointment in a quick glance. Especially because my week has been packed with work, family, and, of course, training.

I’m already getting some supportive tweets right on my wrist, which is really nice. I’m thankful, because training can be tough after a long flight and going from hot to cold weather in a single day. I’ve been following up on email and Instagram from the trip, and I keep thinking back to the mothers I traveled with and others we met in Tanzania through Every Mother Counts. It all reaffirms why I run.

Speaking of running — this was a challenging week. Eight miles Thursday, then 16 on Saturday. I never run that far unless I’m training for a marathon. Now it’s starting to feel real. To finish in 3.59:59 or under, I need to run each mile in 9:09 or less. I ran my fastest marathon at 9:19 per mile, which means I need to shave off 10 seconds. It sounds like a small amount, but it’s going to be hard, and I can’t start out too fast or I’ll burn out. During the 16-miler, I used Apple Watch to keep my pace consistent at a little over 9 minutes per mile the whole way. I was happy because I didn’t get too tired at the end. I’m beginning to think my goal of under 4 hours may actually be doable.

I couldn’t wait to get to a yoga class after all the flying I’ve been doing. So that’s how I started my workout schedule back in New York. Yoga is a perfect addition to all the running I am doing. It keeps me flexible, and strengthens my body in ways that running doesn’t. On Wednesday I took a class at Barry’s Bootcamp, which has a studio right near me. Each session combines intense treadmill cardio and weights. It complements my marathon training perfectly, and also keeps things interesting. And I love the energy of a group, especially when members of Team EMC join me. For these types of workouts, I set the Workout app to Other, because I don’t want to miss the chance to fill my Exercise ring.

Next week, the challenge is to keep up my running during our family vacation. Where we’re staying, it’s hard to find stretches of road long enough to get my distance in. One thing is certain: I can’t stop training. So I think a few treadmill runs may be in store for me.
